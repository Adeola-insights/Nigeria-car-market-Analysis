# Nigeria-car-market-Analysis
An exploratory data analysis of the Nigerian used car market, uncovering consumer preferences, pricing patterns, and geographic distribution across 2,894 vehicle listings. This project demonstrates end-to-end data analysis skills from data cleaning to interactive dashboard creation.          
# Project Overview
This project explores key trends in the Nigerian automobile market by analyzing vehicle pricing, specifications, brand popularity, and regional distribution. The analysis focuses on understanding how dealerships optimize inventory and how customers make purchasing decisions in a price-sensitive, reliability-driven market.
Insights are presented through a structured dashboard designed to support business strategy and consumer behavior analysis.
# Objectives
* Analyze vehicle pricing and condition across the Nigerian market
* Identify dominant brands and fast-moving inventory
* Understand customer preferences around transmission, colour, and performance
* Provide dealership-focused insights for inventory and pricing decisions
* Examine regional demand patterns
# Key Metrics (KPIs)
Average Vehicle Price: ₦5.0M
Average Mileage: 243.3K km
Average Horsepower: 217 hp
Average Engine Size: 3.1L
These metrics reflect a market dominated by used, mid-range vehicles built for durability and long-term value rather than luxury appeal.
#  Market Insights
1️⃣ Vehicle Condition & Pricing Behavior
High average mileage confirms that Nigeria’s car market relies heavily on imported used vehicles (tokunbo). The moderate price point indicates a value-driven market where affordability and longevity matter more than premium features.
Business Insight:
Dealers benefit from faster turnover by focusing on affordable, durable vehicles rather than high-end inventory.
# Brand Dominance & Inventory Strategy
Toyota clearly dominates the market, outperforming other brands such as Honda, Hyundai, Lexus, and Mercedes-Benz. Its dominance is driven by reliability, availability of spare parts, and low maintenance costs.
Business Insight:
Stocking high-demand brands reduces holding time and inventory risk.
# Transmission Preference
Automatic vehicles account for approximately 94% of the market, while manual cars represent a small fraction.
Business Insight:
Manual vehicles have slower sales cycles and often require price discounts.
# Colour Preference & Resale Value
Neutral colours such as black, white, and grey dominate the market.
Business Insight:
Neutral colours improve resale speed and reduce inventory risk.
# Regional Demand Distribution
Vehicle concentration is significantly higher in southern Nigeria, reflecting stronger purchasing power, higher urbanization, and better road infrastructure.
Business Insight:
Dealerships in southern regions experience higher sales velocity.
the Nigerian car market rewards inventory efficiency over variety. Fast-moving brands, automatic transmissions, and neutral colours reduce sales cycles and improve cash flow.
# Tools 
Microsoft Excel – Data cleaning, pivot tables, KPI calculations
Dashboard Design – Visual storytelling and insight communication
# Conclusion
The Nigerian automobile market is defined by practical decision-making. Both dealerships and customers prioritize reliability, affordability, and convenience. Understanding these patterns enables better inventory planning, pricing strategy, and customer targeting within the market.
